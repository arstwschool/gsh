Impact on Other Schools
As the flagship institution for this initiative, Hui-Wen High School's successful project has inspired other schools to join our ranks. Currently, in Taichung City, Hui-Wen High School and Taichung Girls' High School are actively participating. Mingdao High School and Wen-Hwa High School have also been in contact with the People's Peace Foundation, expressing interest. However, due to manpower constraints, there weren't enough people to assist in meal distribution, and the process was interrupted during the pandemic.

In the neighboring Changhua County, schools such as Changhua Arts High School and Cheng Cheng High School have drawn inspiration from the success of Hui-Wen High School's case. They are collaborating with the Changhua Peace Station, following the model set by Hui-Wen High School.

The officers conducted interviews with the foundation and found that, aside from the station head, homeless individuals contribute by preparing the ingredients. Due to economic difficulties or physical disabilities preventing them from working outside, the foundation employs them to distribute meals to those in need. Friends shared that encountering homeless individuals did not evoke judgmental glances, as their appearances were no different from ours. Having the opportunity to converse with them, they would express gratitude and encourage them to face their challenges with courage.

The school teachers are highly supportive, handling complex paperwork and file management on their own. When encountering obstacles, they offer constructive guidance. Looking ahead, the project leaders at Ching Cheng High School express a desire to conduct more on-campus promotions. They aim to expand awareness beyond just volunteers and a few officers, emphasizing that this initiative is a form of social participation.
