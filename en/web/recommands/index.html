Proposal
Our Suggestions to the School Administration:
  • Perhaps consider adding information about the health benefits of specific foods on the lunch notification sheets.
  • Can the portion of meat be increased?
  • Add a lunch portion feedback section on the school website for direct online responses regarding insufficient portions.
  • We hope to return to using iron trays for meals.
  • Aim to improve overall quality, including moisture content and saltiness.
  • Increase portions of special staples (dumplings, noodles, etc.) as those towards the end often miss out.
  • Take into account the consideration of avoiding prolonged food placement.
  • We would like to request the cancellation of vegetarian days.
  • Consider incorporating seasonal and cost-effective fruits.
  • Provide more boneless fish options.
  • Is it possible for the school to independently increase the lunch budget (with students contributing) to enhance quality?
  • There seems to be an uneven distribution between strongly flavored and mild-tasting foods.
  • Increase or decrease the appearance frequency on the favorite/dislike list.
  • Adjust portion sizes based on the gender ratio (provisionally set at 1.5 servings/person for males and serving/person for females).

  • We design the portions of the six major food categories based on the Ministry of Education's regulations on school lunch food content and nutritional standards. It is suggested that each class assigns a student to serve food to ensure an even distribution of meat portions.
  • The lunch contractor has placed inquiry forms for necessary supplies in the preparation area, which are collected on the same day. Moreover, class lunch coordinators are urged to accurately complete and submit weekly satisfaction survey questionnaires to the lunch center (currently with less than half returned).
  • The outsourced dishwashing company currently does not have a dedicated dishwasher for cleaning stainless steel utensils, resulting in poor cleaning and hygiene. Therefore, Corningware is used for better cleaning effectiveness.
  • Please specify which dish is being referred to in the satisfaction survey questionnaires for each class.
  • It is recommended that each class assigns a student to serve food to ensure an even distribution of main course portions.
  • It is unclear which dishes are prone to discoloration. Currently, vegetables are considered for discoloration issues and are cooked last to minimize exposure time.
  • In accordance with the letter No. 1020023751 from the Department of Education of Taichung City on April 10, 2013, all schools are requested to implement vegetarian nutrition lunches every Monday.
  • The Taichung City Government subsidizes students with NT$10 for fruit expenses per week. Our school provides fruit twice a week, mainly using seasonal fruits.
  • Most fish served now have no bones. However, to increase variety, larger fish bones are provided (excluding elementary school children).
  • According to the regulations for lunch supply in senior high schools and below in Taichung City, the school lunch supply committee decides on the school fee standards. The lunch fee was increased by NT$7 in the 112th academic year to maintain lunch quality (including outsourced cleaning costs).
