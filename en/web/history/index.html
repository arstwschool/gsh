History
The Motivations of The Founders
The initiators of the project were SYUAN-YI SHIH and CHUN-YAN YANG, who were students at the Junior High Department of Hui-Wen High School then. They participated in the Youth Creative Camp organized by the library director CHI-HUA TSAI in December 2015. The camp encouraged students to "propose to the world." Inspired by this, SYUAN-YI SHIH and CHUN-YAN YANG envisioned that the leftover food from daily nutritious lunches could become a considerable amount of kitchen waste or serve as food for pigs. Feeling it was a pity, they believed it could be better utilized and thus conceived the "The Sacred Food for the Homeless Project" with the motto "Transforming Leftovers into Sacred Food."

SYUAN-YI's inspiration was closely linked to his childhood experiences helping with farming tasks at his grandfather's house. Watching vegetables grow, he understood that not every seed would thrive, leading him to deeply appreciate the saying, "Treasure every grain of rice and every mouthful of food."
The Initial Challenges of the Sacred Food for the Homeless Project
However, in the early stages of the project, difficulties abounded. Firstly, the school had a contract with a pig farm, agreeing to sell kitchen waste to them for 16,000 NTD annually. However, with the implementation of‚ÄúThe Sacred Food for the Homeless Project‚Äù, the amount of kitchen waste reduced by approximately one-fourth. Consequently, the contract was renegotiated, and the amount was lowered to 12,000 NTD, resolving part of the issue.

Next, identifying students willing to take on the responsibility of collecting leftovers was not an easy task. The founders of the project figured out a way, giving all the volunteers certificates as the credits to apply to universities. However, the hygiene and safety of the leftovers was still a main concern, which made the school hesitate to approve them due to potential risks. After overcoming numerous obstacles, the "People's Peace Foundation" was finally secured during the summer vacation of 2016 to undertake this significant responsibility.

Finally, the school had to avoid unclear liability in case of risks that could jeopardize the school's interests and reputation. Director CHI-HUA TSAI first signed an agreement with the head of the People's Peace Foundation, ensuring that in the event of food poisoning or other hygiene issues, the foundation would take full responsibility, absolving Hui-Wen High School of any involvement. Once this aspect was settled on the school's end, the project could finally proceed to implementation.
Information Related to The Cooperative Foundation
The establishment at first was aimed at helping the impoverished. The brand logo depicts one person supporting another, symbolizing mutual assistance. Applying Maslow's hierarchy of needs, the foundation initially focused on addressing their physiological needs. During times of extreme hunger and cold, even securing basic sustenance was challenging, leaving individuals with little energy to seek employment.

Therefore, volunteers would distribute meals at locations where homeless people gathered. In the early stages, there were few donations of ingredients, and the foundation bore the cost of the food. Later on, generous individuals began providing food, and Hui-Wen High School established contact with the People's Peace Foundation. This collaboration ensured that fluctuations in food prices wouldn't result in homeless individuals missing meals. Students at the school would collect the meals, and the foundation would then transport and store them in insulated containers with lids. The containers would be opened just before distributing the meals. Previously, concerns arose about potential quality deterioration due to temperature changes, especially during the summer.

However, over the seven years of operation, there have been no issues with the food's quality. The head of the Taichung Peace Station explained that their kitchen undergoes daily cleaning, and the Hui-Wen meal service team diligently monitors the quality of ingredients. This meticulous process provides reassurance to those receiving the meals. The quantity of leftover food collected varies each day but is generally sufficient to feed around fifty to sixty individuals, and when quantities allow, meals can even be taken to-go.
Media Coverage
After media coverage from outlets such as the United Daily News, China Times, and other print media, the two initiators were recognized with the Mandarin Daily's 2016 "Good Young" Award in the "Social Engagement" category. Subsequently, their story caught the attention of Public Television (PTS), and filming began in early 2017. The documentary aired nationwide in March, gaining significant traction on Facebook with over 10,000 likes, 700,000 views, and nearly 10,000 shares within two days. Even celebrity Shu Qi shared the video on her Facebook, praising, "Not wasting food, good job, students!" As of today, the video has accumulated 2.37 million views, making it the most impactful episode in the PTS series on food and agricultural education.

Team Leader of This Project
The team leader reminisces about the most significant challenge faced at the project's inception ‚Äì recruiting volunteers. At that time, the officers had recently become acquainted with each other, making them unfamiliar with others. Drafting recruitment forms consumed much time, dealing with incomplete information and overlooked details. The forms were repeatedly sent back for revisions by the director, leading to a moment of self-doubt. The team leader questioned whether they should have taken on such a crucial role.

However, upon reflection, being a second-year student, it was time to take responsibility. They committed to persisting in their role, especially since the school's social studies textbook cited Hui-Wen's Sacred Food Project as one of the reasons for joining meal collection initiatives. Looking ahead, the hope is to expand beyond Hui-Wen High School and a few primary schools, aiming for a larger-scale operation. The belief is that this initiative can provide sustenance to many people.

A message from the officers to their junior peers emphasizes the scheduling of shifts on both odd and even weeks. Even when officers are busy or unable to attend, spontaneous meal collection still occurs, documented through photos sent to the officers via mobile phones. This dedication is considered a heartwarming gesture, and gratitude is expressed to everyone for their collective effort.

Finally, the opportunity for Hui-Wen High School to actively participate in such a meaningful community service project is considered an honor. Experiencing the challenges faced by those living on the streets fosters empathy, contributing to a more compassionate perspective on the world. The wish is for there to always be kind-hearted individuals silently contributing to corners of the world, embracing the Earth with love.
Most Memorable Experience
The head of the Taichung Peace Station recalls his most memorable experience, serving an individual case from Taichung University of Education. This person, once an assistant teacher, faced family upheavals that led to homelessness. At one point, he was so hungry that he slept at the post office. Generous individuals provided him with milk and bread, giving him the strength to reach the People's Peace Foundation seeking help. The station head was deeply moved, realizing that a single meal provided had invisibly assisted many people. In light of this, he expressed to the homeless that they need not hesitate to come to the Taichung Peace Station for a meal. All they have to do is leave their name, with no need to disclose any other information.
