Solution to Dilemmas
Therefore, in 2023, leveraging the newly introduced bonus system, we hope to attract students from other grades to join. The bonus system was proposed by the current officers after noticing a decline in enthusiasm as the project progressed. Without substantial benefits and adequate motivation, meal collections were not as active. Consequently, the team leader proposed this idea to the school director. The proposal suggests awarding bonuses to the top three contributors from the Junior and Senior High Departments each semester. The more collections, the higher the bonus. Currently, the bonuses are set at 1000 NTD for the first place, 800 NTD for the second place, and 500 NTD for the third place. It is anticipated that these amounts will be increased in the future.

Nutritious Lunch Cooking Process
The school's nutritious lunch program is a public-private partnership, with nutritionists playing a supervisory role. The first line of defense in ensuring food quality is the inspection of ingredients. This involves assessing the appearance, checking the texture and temperature by touch to ensure they are within normal ranges. Weight, expiration dates, and adherence to relevant certifications are also part of the inspection process.

Once the inspection is complete, the ingredients are handed over to the preprocessing staff. Since most of the ingredients are already pre-cut, only a small portion is handled by the lunch kitchen. This facilitates the use of a three-sink system for washing. Vegetables and meat undergo cleaning in separate sinks. Subsequently, the chef takes charge of cooking these ingredients, following the sequence from soup to side dishes to the main course. The final step involves blanching the vegetables.
Hygiene-related Regulations and Inspection Process
The cooking process adheres to the standards of good hygiene practices and operates under the Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points (HACCP) control system, which includes temperature control. Ingredients selected are sourced from Taiwan's excellent agricultural products, organic farms, and those with Traceable Agricultural Product (TAP) records, ensuring traceability in production. There is no preference for specific brands; adherence to contract-defined certifications and the mentioned standards is sufficient. To enhance hygiene, the kitchen is located in the basement. The procurement of better equipment, such as exhaust fans, aims to create a more optimal working environment for staff and provide a more reassuring dining experience.
Role of a Nutritionist
The nutritionist sees the greatest challenge in our school being a complete middle and high school, where high school students tend to be more discerning about changes in ingredients and flavors compared to their middle school counterparts. Creating a menu that ensures students perceive the school lunch as not only nutritious but also appetizing and healthy requires thoughtful consideration. The nutritionist plays a crucial role in ensuring the safety and hygiene of meals for the entire school community.

In addition to menu planning, the nutritionist is involved in nutritional education advocacy through materials like pamphlets, setting up booths during school celebrations for nutritional education, and conducting educational lectures each semester. The feedback from students in these activities is considered a meaningful accomplishment by the nutritionist.

Recognizing that students' nutritional knowledge can be influenced by television and peers, the nutritionist believes it is important to empower students to understand food safety and hygiene. By providing practical applications in daily life and encouraging students to make informed choices based on accurate knowledge, the nutritionist aims to foster a better understanding of nutrition.

Furthermore, the nutritionist appreciates initiatives like the Sacred Food Project, where excess food is redistributed, aligning with the concept of a food bank. This approach encourages better utilization of surplus food, contributing to a more sustainable and compassionate environment.
Students Feedback
The lunch kitchen gathers information from both the amount of kitchen waste collected and satisfaction surveys from each class. This allows us to understand which foods are more favored by students. Striving for a balance between nutritional balance and students' preferences, the goal is to effectively achieve portion control. However, on Wednesdays, designated as vegetarian days, many students opt for hot food items, resulting in more kitchen waste. The nutritionist acknowledges that this situation is challenging to avoid but is committed to adjusting the menu accordingly.

In addressing food waste, an alternative proposed solution is to allow students to decide on the menu for certain days. However, implementation is challenging, as the menu needs to be finalized two weeks in advance, and aligning the student-selected menu with the health standards set by the Ministry of Education poses difficulties. Nevertheless, for special occasions such as school celebrations and Christmas, three meal options are designed for each class to vote on, and the menu for the day is determined based on the vote percentages, ensuring student involvement and satisfaction.
